Foundations reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  1. I am prudent. I knew I was cautious and often felt bad about myself for overthinking things, but I have found I am also prudent, which is connected. I like to think through things before I do them. I don’t like to make rushed decisions. I like to step back and look at a problem before attempting it.
  2. I like to do things well, so that I feel I have accomplished something. This makes me work hard and helps with motivation.
  3. I procrastinate because I’m afraid that I won’t be able to do something. I have learnt I can stop procrastinating by facing my fear, or looking at what I’m afraid of. If I tell myself the code doesn’t need to be amazing on the first go, I can draft code and go back to it later.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Empathy and self-awareness are both part of our emotional intelligence, and stem from understanding how we are feeling and how others are feeling. Empathising with other people allows us to be able to relate to them and work better within a team. Self-awareness allows us to regulate our responses to our emotions. For example, if I’m aware that I get anxious or stressed when I am trying to do too many things at once, I can keep an eye on my environment for stress factors and on my physical state (whether I’m feeling tense and hunching my shoulders) to reduce those stress factors before they become overwhelming.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

That it is practical and can be applied to my own life.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Overcoming my bias to things like meditation, which I felt was a bit of an airy-fairy idea and would not be useful, only take up time (which I don’t have much of).

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

It doesn’t feel like a waste of time, because it has given me an insight into who I am and what I need to work on going forward. However I have found it more challenging than coding because it involves introspection and it isn’t always pleasant to question who you are.
