Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I had to make an ethical decision in a previous job when I was asked to back-date signed documents. I was relatively green and I didn’t have enough experience to know whether back-dating was common practice, however I felt uncomfortable doing so because I value honesty and it felt fraudulent. My decision was to leave the job - I was not confident enough to address the practice with my employer.

Looking back on this now, I still feel I was right to be uncomfortable. Having worked further in the field, I know back-dating signed documents isn't normal. I do sometimes wonder if instead of leaving without saying anything, I could have talked to my employer and if that would have changed the way we worked. However I also acknowledge that there was a large power imbalance.

I hope that if I am asked to do something I’m not comfortable with, I will have the bravery and self-certainty to stand up for myself and ask questions. I would like to learn ways to say no when I’m not comfortable with a situation without being confrontational or escalating the situation.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My upbringing influenced my value and identity. My parents believed that technology such as computers and television were bad for my siblings and I. We didn't watch tv or have a computer at home until I was 15.

I hated it at the time as I felt different from my schoolmates, but it did mean that we spent more time making things. We were given off-cuts of wood and hammers/nails/clamps/wood glue and we made a wooden toy castle that we painted. We painted and we drew. I enjoyed and still enjoy creating things. This is what I’m looking forward to most about this course - that we can use code to make something new.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I am prudent: I think through what I’m about to do before doing it.

I am used to high pressure environments: I have learned to deal with stress so that it doesn’t make me rush my work and make stupid mistakes. I have developed methods of managing workloads, for example by looking through a project to see what time it is expected to take and setting daily goals about what I need to complete by when.

I am loyal and kind, and a good teamplayer: I like supporting and working with other people. I get satisfaction from helping others.

I value justice and honesty: I am honest about what work I feel I can complete and I try to divide the workload fairly when working in a team.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I struggle to say no and to stand up for myself and my ideas. This means that I often take on too much work and struggle rather than admitting that I have reached my limit. I also procrastinate and put off starting tasks that I feel are hard or insurmountable.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I was working with three people on a project where none of us knew each other. It was a school project and there were different levels of motivation. One of the team members didn't hold up their portion of the work. At the time, I didn't address the issue. The two of us who were motivated split the other person's work between us. It meant that the two of us had a lot more to do.

If I was in that situation now, I would address the problem with the person who wasn't contributing. If they didn't step up, I would assess what this meant for our group (if it would affect our ability to hand in the project on time) and report it to whoever was overseeing the project.
