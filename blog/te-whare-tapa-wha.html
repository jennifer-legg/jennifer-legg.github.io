Te Whare Tapa Whã

Below is my well-being plan using the Te Whare Tapa Whā system by Tā Mason Durie.

Wall Activity
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Going to the gym for an hour before settling in to do course work on Mon/Tues/Thurs/Fri.
Starting to get ready for bed around 9.30pm because it takes me a while to settle to sleep.
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Spending one afternoon out doing a fun activity on a weekend with both my husband and daughter.
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Listening to the body scan UCLA mindful meditation before sleeping.
Going out for a short five/ten min walk around the block if I'm stuck and frustrated with a particular problem.
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Listening to music from my childhood while I'm studying, to cut the silence and remind me of my family.
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Sitting outside with a cup of tea or coffee when I first get up in the morning, and listening to the birds starting their day.
